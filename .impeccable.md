# Impeccable Design Guide: Soundarya

## 🌟 Vision & Personality
**Brand Personality (3 Words):** `Honest. Precise. Elevated.`

Soundarya is a premium aesthetic analysis platform where ancient wisdom meets modern computational science. It avoids flattery in favor of "confronting but kind" truth.

### User Archetypes (18-34)
- **The Improver**: Seeking a concrete roadmap for enhancement.
- **The Creator**: Seeking social status and "TikTok-ready" validation.
- **The Curious**: Seeking pure entertainment and curiosity.

## 🎨 Visual Identity: "Luxury Editorial"
**Reference Style:** *Vogue digital presence meets high-end watch craftsmanship.*

### Core Aesthetic Rules
- **Mode**: **Permanent Premium Dark only.** Darkness *is* the luxury. No light mode.
- **Geometry**: Sharp corners, thin borders (`1px`), and deliberate, generous spacing. Avoid rounded everything.
- **Palette**: `Deep Black` (#0D0A07) with `Gold` (#C9A96E) accents. Gold should feel expensive/editorial, never electric/neon.
- **Shadows**: Large, soft, deep shadows (`shadow-premium`) to create depth without generic card effects.

### 🚫 Anti-References (DO NOT USE)
- **Gaming/Esports**: No neon on black, no particle effects, no "loading" glows.
- **B2B Tech/SaaS**: No `Inter` font, no blue accents, no Stripe-like dashboard cards, no floating laptop mockups.
- **Wellness/Meditation**: No soft pastels, no watercolor textures, no "bubble" UI.

## 🕹️ Interaction & UX
- **The Core Loop**: Anticipation (Upload) → Suspended Tension (Analysis) → Productive Honesty (Reveal).
- **Feedback**: Results must feel like a consultation with a luxury aesthetician, not a Buzzfeed quiz.
- **Motion**: Use `/animate` to create suspended tension during loading. Avoid "cheap" reveal animations; favor sophisticated, editorial transitions.

## 🛠️ Implementation Tokens
- **Background**: `bg-deep` (#0D0A07)
- **Cards**: `surface-card` (Thin borders, premium shadows)
- **Typography**: `font-serif` (Cormorant) for headings/scores, `font-sans` (Josefin) for utility.
- **Border**: `var(--border-light)` for structure, `var(--gold)` for emphasis.
